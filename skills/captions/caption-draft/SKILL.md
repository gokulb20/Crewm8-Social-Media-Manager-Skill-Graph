# Caption Draft

Write captions for Instagram and LinkedIn that make people stop scrolling, read, and act. Captions are not descriptions of the visual — they're standalone pieces of content that work WITH the visual but deliver value on their own.

## When to Use

- Visual asset (carousel, image, video) is ready and needs a caption
- Instagram feed post, Reel, or Story needs text
- LinkedIn post with an image or carousel needs a supporting caption
- Repurposing a thread or blog into an IG/LinkedIn caption
- Content calendar says "IG post" or "LinkedIn post"

## Quick Reference

| Platform | Ideal Caption Length | Structure | Hashtag Strategy |
|----------|---------------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Instagram feed | 125-150 words (captions can be longer but first 2 lines are critical) | Hook → Value → CTA → Hashtags | 5-10 relevant + 2-3 branded, in caption or first comment |
| Instagram Reels | 50-75 words (video does the heavy lifting) | Hook → Context → CTA | 3-5 |
| Instagram Stories | 1-2 lines + sticker/poll engagement | Quick hit + interactive element | 1-2 |
| LinkedIn | 900-1,200 characters (300-600 words ideal) | Hook → Story/Insight → Lesson → CTA → Hashtags | 3-5 relevant |

## Procedure

1. Start with the visual asset. Look at it. What emotion or idea does it communicate on its own?

2. Write the hook (delegate to `hook-write` if needed):
   - IG: First 2 lines (before "more" expand) must hook. Lead with a question, bold claim, or story opener.
   - LinkedIn: First 2-3 lines before "see more" cut. LinkedIn shows ~210 characters before the cut on desktop, less on mobile.
   - The hook should complement the visual, not describe it. Don't say "Swipe through to see..." — that's filler.

3. Write the body:
   - IG: Value-first. Even long captions should front-load the key insight. Use line breaks for visual rhythm. Write like you talk.
   - LinkedIn: Story structure works best. Setup → conflict/insight → resolution/lesson → applicability.
   - Break long text into single-sentence paragraphs. Walls of text get scrolled past.

4. Write the CTA:
   - IG: "Save this for later," "Share this with someone who needs to hear it," "Comment '[X]' below," "Link in bio"
   - LinkedIn: "Agree? Disagree? Let me know below," "Save for later," "Repost if this resonated"
   - Match CTA intensity to content value (see `cta-craft` skill)

5. Build the hashtag set:
   - IG: 5-10 relevant hashtags + 2-3 branded hashtags. Mix of sizes (1-2 large, 3-5 medium, 3-5 niche).
   - LinkedIn: 3-5 hashtags at the end. All relevant, no fluff hashtags.
   - Place hashtags at end of caption or in first comment (IG only).

6. Do the "would I read this" test:
   - If you saw this in your feed, would you expand the caption?
   - Is the first line interesting enough to stop someone mid-scroll?
   - Does the caption add value beyond what the visual already communicates?

## Output Format

```markdown
# Caption: [Post Title / Visual Description]

## Platform: [Instagram / LinkedIn]

## Caption
[Full caption text, formatted exactly as it should appear on the platform — including line breaks, emoji placement, and hashtag placement]

---
**Hook style:** [Question / Story / Bold claim / Observation / Statistic]
**Body structure:** [Story-led / Framework / List / Single insight]
**CTA:** [CTA type + text]
**Hashtags:** [Number + examples]
**Character count:** [N] characters

## Visual Context
[Brief description of the image/video/carousel this caption accompanies]

## Voice Check
[Does it pass brand voice guide? Any potential voice drifts?]
```

## Platform Notes

**Instagram specifics:**
- No clickable links in captions. Only "link in bio."
- Emojis: 1-3 per caption, used strategically for visual breaks or emotional emphasis
- Carousel captions: the caption is for the whole carousel, but the hook should relate to slide 1 specifically
- Story captions: short, use stickers/polls for engagement, 1-2 hashtags max
- First comment can hold additional hashtags to keep the caption clean

**LinkedIn specifics:**
- No markdown. LinkedIn has its own formatting (bold, italic, bullet lists via UI)
- "Agree?" at the end drives comment engagement, which boosts reach
- Personal stories perform best — even for B2B brands
- Hashtags at the end only, never mid-caption
- "Repost" CTA works better on LinkedIn than other platforms (native repost feature is prominent)

## Pitfalls

- Captions that merely describe what the visual already shows (redundant, adds no value)
- Leading with "Link in bio" or "New post" (weak hooks, wastes the most valuable real estate)
- Instagram captions shorter than 50 characters (looks lazy unless the visual is self-explanatory)
- LinkedIn captions that read like tweets (too short, no depth — LinkedIn rewards substance)
- Hashtag stuffing on LinkedIn (more than 5 looks desperate)
- Forgetting that the Instagram caption first line is the ONLY thing people see before they decide to tap "more"

## Verification

Preview on mobile. Does the first 2 lines make you want to tap "more"? Read the full caption out loud — does it sound human? Would the founder feel good about this being associated with their name? Does the caption add something the visual doesn't?
