# Influencer Outreach

Identify and reach out to micro-influencers and creators for collaboration opportunities. The goal is authentic partnerships with people whose audience overlaps with the brand's target market — not spray-and-pray cold DM spam.

## When to Use

- Building an influencer/creator shortlist for the quarter
- Product launch needs amplification from trusted voices
- Founder says "we should get [type of creator] to talk about us"
- Existing customers include creators who could become partners
- Competitor just launched a successful influencer campaign

## Quick Reference

| Creator Tier | Follower Range | Outreach Approach | Offer Type |
|-------------|---------------|------------------|-----------|
| Nano-influencer | 500-5K | Personal DM, relationship-first | Free product access, affiliate, shoutout swap |
| Micro-influencer | 5K-50K | Personalized email or DM, value-first | Paid collaboration, affiliate, early access |
| Mid-tier | 50K-500K | Professional outreach via email or agent | Paid sponsorship, revenue share |
| Customer-creator | Any size | Relationship already exists — formalize | Ambassador program, affiliate, UGC partnership |

## Procedure

1. Build the target creator list:
   - Mine existing brand followers and engaged commenters (people already interested)
   - Search by niche hashtags and topics (organic discovery)
   - Check who competitors' content is being shared by
   - Look at "building in public" creators in the space
   - Prioritize: audience overlap + engagement quality (not just follower count)

2. Evaluate each candidate:
   - Audience demographics: does their audience match the brand's target?
   - Engagement rate: are people actually talking to this creator? (>2% is good)
   - Content quality: would the brand be proud to be associated with this creator?
   - Brand alignment: does this creator's existing content naturally relate to what the brand does?
   - Past collaborations: have they done brand deals? How did they perform?

3. Build a ranked shortlist (10-20 targets) with:
   - Tier and follower data
   - Best contact method
   - Personal angle (what specific content of theirs you genuinely liked)

4. Draft personalized outreach messages:
   - Reference SPECIFIC content they created (proves you actually follow them)
   - Explain why the brand partnership makes sense (audience overlap, shared values)
   - Be clear about what you're offering and asking
   - Keep it short (creators get spammed with long pitch DMs)
   - No generic "love your content" language (they've heard it 1000 times)

5. Structure the offer:
   - What the brand provides: product access, payment, exposure, affiliate terms
   - What the creator provides: post type, quantity, timeline, usage rights
   - Start with a soft ask: "Would you be open to exploring this?" not "Post this on Tuesday"

6. Founder review and approval before ANY outreach is sent.

7. Track outreach pipeline:
   - Contacted → Opened → Replied → Negotiating → Confirmed → Content live → Performance
   - Follow up once after 7 days if no reply (and only once)
   - After 2 weeks with no response: archive as "no response"

## Pitfalls

- Spraying 100 creators with the same generic DM (low response rate and burns future goodwill)
- Only looking at follower count (a 5K creator with 8% engagement beats a 100K creator with 0.5%)
- Making the offer about what the brand wants, not what the creator gets out of it
- Pitching a creator who just did a deal with a direct competitor (do your research)
- Following up more than once after no response (desperate, annoying)
- Promising things in the DM the founder hasn't approved (budget, terms, timeline)

## Verification

For each outreach draft: if you were this creator, would you reply? Does the message feel like it was written for you specifically, or copied from a template? Is there a genuine personal angle, or just "love your content"? Have you confirmed the founder is okay with every offer before sending?
