# Newsletter to Posts

Turn one newsletter edition into a week's worth of standalone social posts. Newsletters typically contain 3-5 distinct sections, each of which can become its own platform-native post. Stop announcing your newsletter and start atomizing it.

## When to Use

- Newsletter just went out to subscribers
- Back-catalog mining: repurpose old high-performing editions
- Content drought: need posts but no creative bandwidth for net-new ideas
- Newsletter has a section that's too good to stay email-only
- Building an "evergreen content bank" from newsletter archives

## Quick Reference

| Newsletter Section | Best Social Format | Platform |
|-------------------|-------------------|---------|
| Founder's note / personal essay | Story-driven post or short thread | X, LinkedIn |
| Framework / how-to | Thread or carousel | X, LinkedIn |
| Curated links / resources | Thread (one link + insight per tweet) | X |
| Data / metrics breakdown | Stat-led post or thread | X, LinkedIn |
| Reader question / AMA | Engagement post or poll | X, LinkedIn, IG |
| Behind the scenes | Photo + caption or video | IG, TikTok |
| Tool recommendation | Short post with personal experience | X, LinkedIn |

## Procedure

1. Read the full newsletter edition. Highlight every self-contained section that could work as an independent social post.

2. For each section, determine:
   - Can this stand alone without the reader needing context from the rest of the newsletter?
   - Is this section valuable enough on its own to merit a post?
   - What's the best platform and format for this specific section?

3. For each qualifying section, draft a platform-native post:
   - New hook (do not reuse the newsletter subheading — social hooks work differently)
   - Adapted body (newsletter tone is often slightly more formal than social)
   - Platform-appropriate CTA
   - Add: "From this week's newsletter" or "From the archives" as a soft attribution line (not the hook)

4. Structure the CTAs:
   - At least 1 post should directly promote newsletter signup
   - The rest should soft-promote ("this was from the newsletter — link to subscribe in bio")
   - Don't make every post a newsletter ad (audiences tune out)

5. Sequence the posts across the week:
   - Day 1-2: Best 2 posts (highest standalone value)
   - Day 3: Signup-promo post
   - Day 4-5: Remaining posts
   - Avoid posting all newsletter content back-to-back (looks like a content dump)

6. Include the "read the full edition" link in bio or as a reply, never in the main post body (link avoidance on X, limited linking on IG/TikTok).

## Output Format

```markdown
# Posts from: [Newsletter Edition Title / Date]

## Source
[Newsletter edition link or reference]

## Sections Extracted
1. [Section title] → [Platform + Format]
2. ...

## Posts

### Post 1: [Topic]
**Platform:** [X / LinkedIn / IG / TikTok]
**Format:** [Short post / Thread / Carousel / Video script]
**Source section:** [Which part of newsletter]
**Hook:** [Platform-formatted hook text]
**Body:** [Full post text]
**CTA:** [CTA text]
**Attribution:** "From [newsletter name] — link in bio"

### Post 2: ...
...

## Posting Sequence
- [Day/Time]: Post [N] on [Platform]
- [Day/Time]: Post [N] on [Platform]
...

## Newsletter Signup Prompt
[1 dedicated post that explicitly asks people to subscribe — unique hook, not just "subscribe to my newsletter"]
```

## Pitfalls

- Posting the newsletter as-is on social (nobody reads a 2,000-word LinkedIn post that was clearly an email)
- Every post saying "from my newsletter" without delivering standalone value — it's an ad, not content
- Using the same CTA ("subscribe!") on every post — dilutes the ask
- Not checking if the newsletter sections are too "inside baseball" for a cold social audience (subscribers know you, random feed scrollers don't)
- Cannibalizing newsletter value: if social posts cover everything, there's no reason to subscribe

## Verification

For each post: would this make someone who's never heard of the newsletter want to subscribe? If a post is so complete that subscribing feels unnecessary, cut it back to a teaser with a clear "rest is in the newsletter" gap.
