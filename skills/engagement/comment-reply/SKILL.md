# Comment Reply

Draft replies that make people feel heard on your own posts and make the brand visible in other people's feeds. The comment section is where community is built — one thoughtful reply is worth more than a generic "thanks!"

## When to Use

- Daily engagement routine (check and reply to all comments on brand posts)
- High-value accounts in the niche posted something worth engaging with
- Trending post in the space where a brand comment adds visibility
- Founder is building-in-public and other founders are engaging
- Someone shared, reposted, or gave the brand a shoutout

## Quick Reference

| Reply Type | Goal | Tone | Max Length |
|-----------|------|------|-----------|
| Reply to own post comment | Acknowledge, add value, keep conversation going | Warm, conversational | 1-3 sentences |
| Value-add comment on others' posts | Show expertise, be helpful, be visible | Insightful, non-promotional | 2-4 sentences |
| Thank-you for share/shoutout | Express genuine gratitude | Warm, specific (reference what they shared) | 1-2 sentences |
| Disagreement / debate | Disagree respectfully, add perspective | Professional, curious, not combative | 2-4 sentences |

## Procedure

### Part A: Replying to comments on brand posts

1. Review all new comments since the last check (ideally every 2-4 hours during active posting windows).

2. Categorize each comment:
   - Positive/affirming: reply with warmth + add a follow-up thought
   - Question: answer directly and thoroughly
   - Disagreement/debate: acknowledge their point, add perspective, avoid defensiveness
   - Spam/troll: do not engage (escalate to `crisis-respond` if needed)
   - Low-effort ("nice!" "great post"): reply with a short acknowledgment + question to extend conversation

3. Draft replies:
   - Start by acknowledging their specific point (not a generic thanks)
   - Add something: a follow-up question, additional context, or validation
   - Match the energy of their comment (if they wrote a paragraph, don't reply with one word)
   - Stay in brand voice — replies are content too

4. Timing priority:
   - Reply within 1 hour of comment during active posting windows
   - Reply within 4 hours for off-hours comments
   - Early replies boost algorithmic performance (platform sees active conversation)

### Part B: Engaging on other accounts' posts

5. Identify target accounts for the day (from `content-calendar` or a running list):
   - 10-15 high-value accounts in the niche
   - Other founders building-in-public
   - Potential customers' posts
   - Trending posts in the space

6. Draft value-add comments:
   - NEVER promotional (no "check out our product" in someone else's replies)
   - Add insight, ask a thoughtful question, share a relevant experience
   - Be specific: reference something they actually said (proves you read it)
   - Length: 2-4 sentences. Long enough to add value, short enough to be read

7. Quality check:
   - Would this comment make someone curious about who wrote it?
   - Does it add to the conversation or just take up space?
   - If the brand had no product to sell, would this still be a good comment?

## Output Format

```markdown
# Comment Replies: [Date / Session]

## Replies on Brand Posts

### Post: [Post title or hook truncated]
**Commenter:** @handle
**Comment:** "[Their comment text]"
**Reply:** "[Drafted reply]"
**Type:** [Acknowledgment / Question answer / Debate / Low-effort follow-up]

---

## Comments on Other Accounts

### Account: @handle
**Their post:** [Post topic / hook truncated]
**Our comment:** "[Drafted comment]"
**Strategy:** [Building authority / Engaging peer / Supporting community / Visibility]

---

## Daily Engagement Summary
- Brand comments replied to: [N]
- Other accounts engaged: [N] of [N] target
- Unreplied (flagged): [N] — need founder input or escalation
```

## Pitfalls

- Generic replies: "Thanks!" "Great point!" — indistinguishable from a bot
- Self-promotional comments on other accounts: fastest way to get blocked or called out
- Arguing with trolls: don't feed them, escalate to `crisis-respond`
- Commenting just to comment: if you have nothing valuable to add, be silent
- Over-commenting on one account (looks like a fan account, not a peer brand)
- Replying so fast to every comment it looks automated (leave some breathing room)

## Verification

Read your drafted replies. If you replaced the brand name with a random person's name, would these replies still be good? If the answer is yes, they pass. Comments should be valuable as COMMENTS, not as brand positioning.
