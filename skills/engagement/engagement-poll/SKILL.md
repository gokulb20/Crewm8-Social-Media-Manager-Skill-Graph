# Engagement Poll

Design polls, questions, and interactive content that boosts engagement AND generates audience insight. A good poll does double duty: it feeds the algorithm and feeds your understanding of the audience.

## When to Use

- Weekly engagement-boost slot in the content calendar
- Audience research needed (validate product ideas, content preferences, pain points)
- Low-engagement period — poll can reset algorithmic momentum
- Before a product launch (gauge interest, segment audience by response)
- After a controversial post (poll channels the conversation constructively)

## Quick Reference

| Poll Type | Best Platform | Format | Example |
|----------|--------------|--------|---------|
| Binary choice | X, LinkedIn, IG Stories | "A or B?" with explanation context | "Which is harder: getting first 10 customers or first 100?" |
| Preference | IG Stories, X | "Which [X] do you prefer?" | "What content format helps you most: threads, videos, or carousels?" |
| Opinion / Agree-Disagree | LinkedIn, X | Statement + agree/disagree options | "Founders should do customer support for the first year. Agree?" |
| This-or-That | IG Stories, TikTok | Visual + two options | Product mockup A vs B for design feedback |
| Open-ended question | LinkedIn, X | Question + "reply with your answer" | "What's one SaaS metric you track that actually changed a decision?" |
| Ranking | IG Stories (slider), X | "Rate this from 1-10" | "How confident are you in your pricing? 1-10" |

## Procedure

1. Determine the poll's dual purpose:
   - Primary: engagement boost (algorithm)
   - Secondary: audience insight (research)
   - Every poll should serve both, not just one

2. Choose the right platform and format:
   - X: native poll (4 options max, 1 day duration default, can set shorter). Good for opinion/factual questions.
   - LinkedIn: native poll (4 options max, 1-2 weeks duration). Good for professional/industry questions.
   - IG Stories: poll sticker (2 options), question sticker (open-ended), slider, quiz. Best for visual-first engagement.
   - TikTok: Q&A feature, comments-based polling, or interactive video format. Less formal poll structure.

3. Write the poll question:
   - Make it genuinely interesting to the audience (not just interesting to you)
   - Avoid leading questions (you'll only learn what you want to hear)
   - Keep it specific (broad questions get generic answers)
   - Add context: 1-2 sentences explaining why you're asking
   - Poll text + supporting post/caption should make a cohesive unit

4. Write poll options (for native polls):
   - 2 options: simple, high response rate. Best for quick engagement.
   - 3-4 options: more nuanced, slightly lower response. Best for insight gathering.
   - Keep options short (1-3 words each). No full sentences in options.
   - Make all options equally valid (no obviously "right" answer — kills engagement)

5. Plan the follow-up:
   - Poll results post: share results + your analysis (48 hours after poll ends)
   - This creates a 2-post arc: poll → results
   - Tag or DM respondents who had interesting takes (relationship building)

6. For IG Stories specifically:
   - Poll sticker: simple, always works
   - Question sticker: more effort for respondents, higher value responses
   - Slider: fun, quick, works for NPS-style "how much do you agree" questions
   - Quiz: educational, good for reinforcing brand expertise
   - Combine: one story sequence with 2-3 interactive elements

7. Track poll performance:
   - Votes/completions (engagement metric)
   - Result distribution (insight metric)
   - Comments/replies generated (secondary engagement)
   - New followers attributed to poll (if trackable)

## Output Format

```markdown
# Engagement Poll: [Topic / Date]

## Poll Purpose
**Primary:** [Engagement boost / Algorithm reset]
**Secondary:** [Audience insight goal — what do you want to learn?]

## Platform: [X / LinkedIn / IG Stories / TikTok]

## Poll Content

### [Platform] Native Poll
**Question:** [Poll question text]
**Options:**
1. [Option 1]
2. [Option 2]
3. [Option 3] (optional)
4. [Option 4] (optional)
**Duration:** [1 day / 3 days / 1 week]
**Supporting caption/context:** [1-2 sentences explaining why you're asking]

### [For open-ended / non-poll formats]
**Post text:** [Full post asking for replies]
**CTA:** [What you want people to do — reply, vote, DM]

## Follow-Up Plan
- **Results post timing:** [Date/time — ~48 hours after poll closes]
- **Results post angle:** [What insight emerged + your unexpected takeaway]
- **Engagement from results:** [Reply to interesting responses, tag insightful commenters]

## Expected Outcomes
- **Votes/completions target:** [N]
- **Insight hypothesis:** [What you expect the results to show]
```

## Pitfalls

- Polls that are thinly veiled marketing ("Would you buy our product: Yes or Definitely Yes?")
- Asking questions the audience doesn't care about (polls should reflect THEIR world, not your internal debates)
- Never sharing results (polls without follow-up feel unfinished, waste audience participation)
- Poll fatigue: more than 2 poll-style posts per week on any platform (people stop voting)
- IG Story polls where the question is too long to fit on the sticker (keep it under 20 characters if possible)
- Binary polls where both options are basically the same (you'll learn nothing)

## Verification

Ask: would I vote on this poll if I saw it in my feed? Is the question genuinely interesting or just content filler? Will the results actually change anything about how the brand operates (or is it just "engagement for engagement's sake")? Is the follow-up results post planned?
