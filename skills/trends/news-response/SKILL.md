# News Response

Monitor industry news and write fast, thoughtful responses that position the brand as plugged-in and opinionated. Speed wins on news — the first insightful take gets disproportionate engagement. Being third or fourth to comment on a major announcement means you're invisible.

## When to Use

- Major industry announcement (funding round, acquisition, product launch, regulatory change)
- Competitor launched something significant
- Technology breakthrough or trend report published
- Founder sees a news item and says "we should weigh in on this"
- Market or economic event that impacts the brand's audience

## Quick Reference

| News Type | Response Time Target | Format | Tone |
|----------|---------------------|--------|------|
| Major industry news | Within 1 hour | Short post + potential follow-up thread | Insightful, fast analysis |
| Competitor announcement | Within 2-3 hours | Respectful commentary (never dunk on competitors) | Professional, complementary, differentiated |
| Data/trend report | Within 4-6 hours | Thread or LinkedIn post — pull most surprising stats | Analytical, helpful framing |
| Founder/figure controversy | Assess carefully — sometimes silence is best | If engaging: calm, principled stance | Measured, values-driven |
| Good-news-in-the-space | Within 2-4 hours | Celebrate + add context | Genuine, not opportunistic |

## Procedure

1. Set up news monitoring channels:
   - X lists of industry journalists, analysts, and breaking-news accounts
   - Google News alerts for key company/product/industry terms
   - Niche newsletters and Substacks that break news early
   - TechCrunch, The Verge, Axios, and relevant trade publications
   - Competitor social accounts (they announce their own news)

2. When news breaks, immediately assess:
   - Is this genuinely relevant to the brand's audience? (Not all industry news is)
   - Can the brand add a unique perspective? (Not just "here's the news")
   - Is there a time-sensitive window? (First-mover advantage window)
   - Is this news where silence might be smarter? (Controversy, tragedy, legal issues)

3. Draft the response rapidly:
   - Acknowledge the news (1 line — assume your audience hasn't seen it yet)
   - Add the brand's unique take (what does this MEAN for the audience?)
   - Provide context the headline missed (this is where you add value)
   - Frame implications: why this matters, what changes, what to watch
   - CTA: invite discussion, ask for predictions, share related resource

4. Choose the right format:
   - X: fast short post (1-2 tweets) or quote tweet of the news source
   - LinkedIn: longer analysis post (post-news, pre-analysis format)
   - Not typically breaking-news for IG/TikTok (unless visual-first angle)
   - Thread: if the analysis requires 4-6 connected points

5. Apply news response quality controls:
   - Fact check: is the news confirmed or rumor? (Don't spread rumors)
   - Attribution: link to or cite the original source
   - Brand fit: does responding to this make the brand look smart or opportunistic?
   - Tone calibration: serious news gets serious tone; good news gets celebratory

6. Track news response performance:
   - Time-to-post from news break
   - Engagement compared to average posts (news responses should overperform)
   - Did the take age well? (Review 48 hours later — did subsequent developments validate or contradict?)

## Output Format

```markdown
# News Response: [Story / Headline]

## Source
**News break time:** [Timestamp]
**Original source:** [URL / @handle]
**News summary:** [One sentence — what happened]

## Assessment
**Relevance to audience:** [High / Medium / Low] — [why]
**Unique angle available:** [Yes / No] — [what angle]
**First-mover window:** [Estimated hours remaining]
**Risk level:** [Low / Medium / High — any controversy potential?]

## Response Draft

### [Platform]
[Full post text, formatted for platform]

**Format:** [Short post / Thread / Quote tweet / Long-form]
**Tone:** [Insightful / Analytical / Celebratory / Measured / Critical]
**CTA:** [What action to drive]

## Post-Response Tracking
- **Posted at:** [Timestamp]
- **Time from news break:** [N] minutes
- **48-hour engagement:** [TBD]
- **Did the take hold up?** [TBD — revisit]
```

## Pitfalls

- Responding to breaking news without a genuine take ("This is big" — no insight)
- Taking a stance on controversial news without founder approval
- Getting facts wrong because speed was prioritized over accuracy
- Quoting or sharing competitor news in a way that accidentally promotes them
- Responding to every piece of news (brand becomes a news aggregator, not a thought leader)
- Being the 14th person with the same take on the same story (late + unoriginal = double invisible)

## Verification

Before posting: is this take something only this brand could say? (Or could anyone with a LinkedIn account have written it?) Is the take more insightful than the top 3 existing responses to this news? If not, refine until it is, or don't post.
