# Content Pillars

Transform the brand's positioning into 3-5 repeatable content categories (pillars). Each pillar is a lens through which the brand talks about the world. The taxonomy underneath ensures the agent never runs out of specific ideas.

## When to Use

- After positioning is locked in, before building the first calendar
- Content feels repetitive or scattered
- Quarterly content strategy refresh
- Expanding into a new platform (pillars may flex per platform)
- Founder says "I don't know what to post about"

## Quick Reference

| Pillar Type | Purpose | Example Topics |
|-------------|---------|---------------|
| Education / How-to | Teach the audience something valuable | Frameworks, step-by-step guides, mistakes to avoid |
| Point of View / Contrarian | Share a differentiated opinion | Hot takes, industry myths, "unpopular opinion" |
| Proof / Social Proof | Show that the thing works | Case studies, results, customer stories, revenue updates |
| Behind the Scenes / Build-in-Public | Humanize the brand | Team culture, process, failures, lessons learned |
| CTA / Conversion | Direct audience to take action | Product launches, demos, newsletter signups, waitlists |

## Procedure

1. Start with the positioning statement. For each pillar, ask: "Does content in this pillar reinforce the brand's core message?"

2. Define 3-5 pillars. Each pillar gets:
   - A name (1-3 words)
   - A one-sentence description of what content in this pillar does
   - 10-15 specific topic ideas (examples, not templates)
   - The target split (e.g., 40% education, 25% POV, 20% proof, 10% BTS, 5% CTA)

3. For each pillar, define the "content formats" that work best:
   - Education: threads, carousels, how-to videos
   - POV: short takes, quote tweets, contrarian threads
   - Proof: case study posts, metric screenshots, testimonial graphics
   - BTS: photo + caption, process videos, raw updates
   - CTA: launch announcements, link posts, demo clips

4. Map each pillar to platforms:
   - Not every pillar works equally on every platform
   - Example: BTS dominates IG/TikTok, Education dominates LinkedIn, POV dominates X

5. Create the pillar mix schedule (what ratio per week for each platform).

6. Build a "pillar health check" — if one pillar is getting all the engagement and another is dead, flag an adjustment.

## Output Format

```markdown
# [Brand] Content Pillars

## Pillar 1: [Name]
**Goal:** [What this content achieves]
**Split:** [X% of weekly output]
**Formats:** [Thread, carousel, short post, etc.]
**Platform priority:** [X > LinkedIn > IG > TikTok or custom order]

### Topic Bank (15 ideas)
1. [Specific topic]
2. [Specific topic]
...

## Pillar 2: [Name]
...

## Weekly Mix
| Day | Platform | Pillar | Format |
|-----|----------|--------|--------|
| Mon | X | Education | Thread |
| Mon | LinkedIn | Education | Carousel |
| Tue | X | POV | Short post |
...

## Pillar Health Check
Review monthly:
- Which pillar drives the most engagement?
- Which pillar drives the most conversions?
- Is any pillar underrepresented or overperforming?
```

## Pitfalls

- More than 5 pillars dilutes everything — the audience can't tell what you stand for
- Pillars that are too broad ("Business Advice") are useless as a filter
- Same pillar mix on every platform — X is not LinkedIn is not TikTok
- Forgetting that CTA content needs the other 4 pillars as scaffolding (you earn the right to ask)

## Verification

Map the last 2 weeks of posts to pillars. If more than 20% of posts don't fit any pillar, the taxonomy is wrong. If one pillar exceeds 60% of output, the mix needs rebalancing.
