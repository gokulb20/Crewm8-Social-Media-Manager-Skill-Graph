# Positioning Statement

Define the core positioning that every piece of content ladders up to. This is the answer to "what do you do?" that lives in the background of every post, thread, and reply.

## When to Use

- Before building a content calendar (content without positioning is noise)
- Founder's messaging is scattered across platforms
- After a pivot, rebrand, or new product launch
- Quarterly positioning refresh
- New team member needs the one-sentence north star

## Quick Reference

| Element | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Target audience | Who exactly are you talking to? (Not "everyone") |
| Problem | What specific pain point do you solve? |
| Solution | How do you solve it, in one sentence? |
| Differentiator | Why you and not the 47 other options? |
| Proof | What evidence backs this up? |

## Procedure

1. Review existing materials: website, pitch decks, founder interviews, investor updates, customer testimonials.

2. Identify the target audience with precision:
   - Role, company stage, specific pain, aspiration
   - Example: "Seed-stage SaaS founders who are technical but hate marketing"

3. Articulate the problem in their words:
   - Use language customers actually use, not marketing speak
   - Find verbatim quotes from support tickets, sales calls, reviews

4. Define the solution in one plain sentence:
   - No jargon. If your mom wouldn't understand it, rewrite it.

5. Identify the sharpest differentiator:
   - Speed? Price? Approach? Specific use case? Philosophy?
   - "We're the X for Y" is a valid shortcut if it's accurate

6. List proof points: metrics, customer logos, testimonials, case studies.

7. Write 3 positioning statement variants:
   - The elevator pitch (1 sentence, any context)
   - The social bio version (150 characters, platform-ready)
   - The contrarian take (what belief does the brand hold that competitors don't?)

8. Stress-test: can every piece of planned content for the next month ladder up to this positioning?

## Output Format

```markdown
# [Brand] Positioning

## Elevator Pitch
[One sentence — who you help, what you do, why it matters]

## Social Bio Version
[150 characters max — the X/LinkedIn/IG bio version]

## Contrarian Take
[The belief that sets you apart from the default narrative in your space]

## Target Audience
- Primary: [specific persona]
- Secondary: [adjacent persona, if any]

## Problem They Feel
[In their words]

## How We Solve It
[One sentence, no jargon]

## Why Us
[Sharpest differentiator]

## Proof Points
- [Metric / logo / testimonial]

## Content Litmus Test
Can every post this month trace back to one of these ideas?
```

## Pitfalls

- Positioning that's true but not interesting ("we help businesses grow" — everyone says this)
- Positioning that's narrow enough nobody cares (too niche)
- Copying a competitor's positioning because it looks good on paper
- Positioning that changes with every content pillar — it should be the anchor

## Verification

Show the 3 variants to 5 people who fit the target audience. Ask: "What do you think this company does?" If 3+ people can't explain it back in their own words, the positioning isn't clear enough.
